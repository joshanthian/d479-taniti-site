About Taniti

Discover the Heart of Taniti

Nestled in the warm embrace of the Pacific Ocean, Taniti is a hidden gem spanning under 500 square miles. This tropical paradise boasts diverse landscapes—from soft sandy shores and rugged rocky beaches to vibrant rainforests and a majestic, active volcano at its core. Home to an indigenous population of around 20,000, Taniti's culture thrives on tradition, hospitality, and harmony with nature. While fishing and agriculture once fueled its economy, the island is now gracefully stepping into a bright future led by tourism—inviting visitors from around the world to explore its rich beauty and welcoming spirit.

Frequently Asked Questions

🔌 What kind of power outlets are used in Taniti?

Taniti uses standard 120-volt power outlets—the same as in the United States.

🍹 What are the alcohol laws on the island?

Alcohol cannot be served or sold between midnight and 9:00 a.m. The legal drinking age is 18, though enforcement is generally relaxed.

🗣️ Will I be able to speak English on the island?

Yes! Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. In rural areas, especially among older residents, English may be less common.

🏥 What kind of healthcare is available?

Taniti has one main hospital and several clinics. The hospital staff includes many multilingual professionals to assist international visitors.

🔒 Is it safe to travel to Taniti?

Yes. Violent crime is extremely rare. However, with the rise in tourism, there has been an increase in petty theft like pickpocketing. Be cautious in crowded areas.

📅 Are there any cultural holidays I should be aware of?

Taniti celebrates numerous national holidays. On these days, many restaurants and tourist spots may be closed—so plan accordingly.

💵 What currency is used?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar. Many businesses also accept euros and yen. Credit cards are widely accepted, and currency exchange is available at several banks.
